## The Road-Bike Reflex
New cyclists walk into a shop and reflexively point at the fastest, lightest road bike on the wall. They picture themselves flying down open roads, not sitting at a red light with a backpack and a pannier. The road bike is built for a ride most commuters never actually take.

## Why Hybrids Commute Better
A hybrid gives you an upright position so you can see traffic instead of staring at your front wheel. The wider tires shrug off potholes and gravel that would rattle a road bike to pieces. For stop-and-go city riding, comfort and control beat raw speed every time.

## Buying Used Wisely
The used market is full of barely-ridden hybrids someone bought on a resolution and abandoned by February. Check the chain for rust, spin the wheels to see if they wobble, and squeeze the brakes before you hand over cash. A three-year-old hybrid at half price will outlast a bargain-bin new bike.

## Outfitting for the City
Add fenders so road spray does not paint a stripe up your back on wet mornings. Bolt on a rear rack and lights, because carrying your bag on your body wrecks the whole point of commuting by bike. A cheap lock you actually use beats an expensive one you leave at home.
