## The Immersion Myth
Everyone tells you to move abroad or binge foreign films until fluency soaks in by osmosis. But immersion without effort is just background noise, and you can spend years surrounded by a language and still not speak it. Passive exposure feels productive because it is comfortable, and comfort is exactly the problem.

## Why Output Drives Fluency
Language sticks when you have to retrieve it, not when you recognize it. Every time you force a sentence out of your own head, you are strengthening the exact skill you actually want. Input tells you what a word means; output teaches you to use it under pressure.

## Forcing Production Early
Start speaking on day one, badly, before you feel ready, because you will never feel ready. Write a clumsy paragraph, record a voice note, order coffee in broken grammar and let the mistakes happen. The errors are the lesson, and you cannot get feedback on words you never say.

## A Weekly Routine
Pick three days for short speaking drills and two days for writing something a native could correct. Keep the sessions to twenty minutes so you actually show up, and always produce more than you consume. End each week by reusing the mistakes from the last one on purpose.
