## You Do Not Need a Spreadsheet
There is a myth that investing means tracking tickers, building budgets in cells, and rebalancing charts every weekend. You can skip all of it. A broad index fund is designed so that doing nothing is the strategy, not a compromise.

## One Fund, Automated
Pick a single total market or S&P 500 index fund and set up an automatic transfer into it every payday. That one boring choice buys you a slice of thousands of companies without you picking a single one. Automation means the decision happens once and then never bothers you again.

## Fees Are the Enemy
The one number that actually matters is the expense ratio, and lower is always better. A fund charging one percent quietly eats a huge chunk of your returns over thirty years, while an index fund charging a fraction of that leaves the growth with you. If you optimize for anything, optimize for paying as little as possible to hold your money.

## Set It and Forget It
Once the automatic transfer is running, the best move is to ignore your account, especially when the market drops. Checking daily just tempts you to sell at the worst time and undo the whole plan. Boring, hands off, and consistent beats clever and anxious almost every time.
