## The Solar Enthusiasm
Homeowners who want to go green almost always start by pricing out rooftop panels. Solar feels like the big visible statement, the thing you can point at from the driveway. But leading with panels usually means paying to power a house that wastes half of what it takes in.

## Efficiency Comes First
Before you generate a single watt, you should stop the ones you are already leaking. Air sealing, insulation, and better windows cut your demand for a fraction of what solar costs. Every dollar spent on efficiency shrinks the system you eventually need to buy.

## Sequencing the Upgrades
Seal the envelope, then upgrade heating and cooling, then look at what load is actually left. By the time you get to panels, you are sizing them for a lean house instead of a drafty one. Doing it in this order can shave thousands off the final array.

## When Solar Makes Sense
Once your home sips energy instead of gulping it, rooftop solar becomes a smart finishing move. A smaller system on an efficient house pays itself back faster than a giant one on a leaky box. Solar is a great last step, and a wasteful first one.
