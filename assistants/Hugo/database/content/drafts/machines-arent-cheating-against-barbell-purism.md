## The Purist Objection
There is a loud camp that says free weights are the only real training and machines are for people who aren't serious. The claim is that barbells build stabilizers and machines let you cheat the hard part. It sounds tough, but it's mostly gym folklore.

## What Machines Do Well
Machines put load on a muscle without demanding balance and technique first. That means you can push closer to failure safely, isolate a lagging muscle, and keep training around an injury. None of that is cheating, it's just controlling the variable you care about.

## Stimulus, Not Ideology
Muscle responds to tension and effort, not to the moral category of the equipment. A hard set on a leg press grows quads the same way a hard squat does. Pick the tool that lets you deliver the stimulus with the least junk fatigue.

## Building a Mixed Program
The strongest programs use both without apology. Anchor your sessions with a few big barbell lifts for skill and total load, then use machines to add volume and hit the muscles those lifts miss. Purity is a worse organizing principle than results.
