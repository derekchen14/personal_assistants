## The Myth of the Lone Inventor
The story everyone learns is that Edison sat down, had a flash of genius, and handed the world electric light. That is not what happened. Incandescent lighting had dozens of people working on it for decades before Edison filed a single patent.

## Swan, Latimer, and the Rivals
Joseph Swan had a working incandescent bulb in England and even lit his own house with it, forcing Edison into a legal fight he eventually settled by merging companies. Lewis Latimer patented a far better carbon filament and made the bulb something you could actually manufacture. These were not footnotes, they were people solving the same problem in parallel and sometimes solving it first.

## What Edison Actually Improved
Edison's real contribution was making the bulb practical and long lasting, and wrapping it in a whole system of generators, wiring, and meters. He and his lab tested thousands of filament materials until they found one that burned for hundreds of hours instead of minutes. That is engineering and relentless iteration, not the single invention the legend claims.

## Why the Story Stuck
We love a clean origin story with one hero, because it is easier to remember than a messy tangle of rivals and lawsuits. Edison was also a brilliant self promoter who understood that owning the narrative was as valuable as owning the patent. So the myth stuck, and the people who got there alongside him faded out of the textbook.
