## The Telescope Mistake
Almost everyone new to the night sky buys a telescope first, and most of those telescopes end up in a closet. They're fiddly to aim, they show a tiny patch of sky, and a cheap one delivers a blurry letdown. The scope isn't the on-ramp to astronomy, it's the thing that scares people off it.

## What Binoculars Reveal
A plain pair of binoculars shows you the craters of the moon, the moons of Jupiter, and star clusters that look like spilled sugar. The wide view makes it easy to find things and to grasp how the sky is laid out. You learn to actually navigate up there before you ever zoom in.

## Choosing a First Pair
You don't need anything exotic, a 7x50 or 10x50 pair does everything a beginner wants. The first number is magnification and the second is the lens size that gathers light, and 50 gathers plenty for night use. Handheld and steady beats powerful and shaky every time.

## Your First Night Out
Go out on a clear night, start with the moon because it's impossible to miss, then swing over to a bright star or the Pleiades. Brace your elbows on something or lean against a wall so the view holds still. One good session like this teaches you more than a season of fighting a telescope.
