## The Raised-Bed Cost Trap
Raised beds get sold as the beginner's starting point, but they are the expensive way in. By the time you buy lumber, hardware, and enough soil to fill a big box, you're hundreds of dollars deep before a single seed goes in. That cost stops a lot of people before they ever grow anything.

## Why Buckets Win
A five-gallon bucket costs a few dollars and grows a shocking amount of food. You can move it to chase the sun, bring it in during a cold snap, and add one at a time as you get hooked. Ten buckets still cost less than one filled raised bed.

## Drainage and Soil
Drill a ring of holes in the bottom of each bucket so roots never sit in water, which is the one mistake that kills container plants. Fill with a light potting mix, not heavy garden dirt, because roots in a bucket need air as much as water. Get those two things right and the plant barely knows it's in a bucket.

## What to Plant First
Start with one tomato, a couple of peppers, and a bucket of leafy greens you can cut again and again. These give fast, obvious wins that keep a new gardener coming back. Master those and you can branch into almost anything a bed would hold.
